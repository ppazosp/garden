Claude Code's source code got leaked. Not some sophisticated exploit, not a flaw in the AI pipeline. Their deploy process had a few manual steps and someone skipped one. Human error.

Boris Cherny, Claude Code's creator, said the fix is counterintuitive: go faster, not add more process. More automation, more checks run by Claude itself. Fewer spots where a person has to remember to do the thing.

Think about it. The guy who said Claude Code writes 100% of his code had his product leak because of the one part a human still touched. You automate everything, and whatever manual step you leave behind becomes your single point of failure. After all the discourse about AI replacing developers, turns out the human was the bug.
